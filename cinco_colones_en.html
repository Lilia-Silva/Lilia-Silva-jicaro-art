Allegory of Coffee and Bananas: A Tribute to Costa Rican History and Art

Inspiration and Historical Significance

This project, inspired by the 5 colones bill from Costa Rica, is by far the largest and most challenging piece of my artistic career. The 5 colones bill is not just currency but a cultural icon, recognized globally as one of the most beautiful banknotes. The image that graces this bill is a reproduction of the painting "Allegory of Coffee and Bananas" by Aleardo Villa, which is housed in the National Theatre of Costa Rica, a national treasure and a masterpiece of architecture.

The allegory depicts Costa Rica's agricultural wealth, with two female figures representing the nation's most important exports in the 19th century: coffee and bananas. This artwork is a tribute to Costa Rica's golden era, where these exports drove the nation's economic growth and financed the construction of the National Theatre, which remains a cultural beacon to this day.

The Artistic Challenge

For me, this project was more than just a reproduction. It was an in-depth exploration of detail and history. My goal was to capture the essence of this masterpiece in my unique style: jícara mosaic. This monumental piece spans nearly 3 meters in length and took over a year to complete, truly testing my patience and dedication.

The process began with the preparation of materials. As usual, I worked with jícara shells, breaking them into small pieces and assembling them one by one. Each fragment was carefully placed to recreate not only the human figures but also the intricate background details, colors, and textures that bring the allegory to life. I used a combination of painted and natural jícara to highlight contrasts within the piece.

The Scale and Details

One of the biggest challenges was working with the sheer scale of the artwork. Capturing the small details of the original bill in such a large format required immense precision. Every mosaic piece had to be meticulously placed to ensure that the overall design reflected the elegance and complexity of the original. The female figures, plants, and ornamental details were recreated with great care, ensuring that the piece retained the grandeur of the original.

Conclusion: A Tribute to Costa Rican Culture

This project is more than just a homage to an artistic piece; it’s a tribute to Costa Rica's history and cultural heritage. The allegory on the 5 colones bill is a symbol of Costa Rican identity, and working on this piece has allowed me to deeply connect with the nation's history and its cultural treasures.

Combining traditional art with the innovative medium of jícara mosaic has allowed me to reinterpret this iconic image in a completely new way. I believe that this project not only celebrates art but also reflects the resilience and dedication needed to complete such a monumental work.
